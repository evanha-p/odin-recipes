Tea

Hot water with some leaves? Well why not!

If you thought I was scraping the barrel with my boiled egg recipe oh boy were you wrong. Tea (the drink) comes from tea leaves that are dried and the usually packed inside handy
pourous pouches (called tea bags) for ease of use. Oh and btw tea leaves themselves grow
on tea plants, I know, a shocker!

There are many varities of teas. Most common ones are green, white, black and yellow tea.
More versions exist and if we also count all the versions that don't necessary have
tea leaves in them but some other plants like ginger or some mixture of spices our options
are almost unlimited. The instructions on this page can be used pretty much with any
kind of tea.

Ingredients

  • Tea variety based on your own preference (I prefer green tea or chai)
  • Water

Steps

  1. Boil water
  2. Pour the hot water into a cup
  3. Add the tea
  4. Let it brew for few minutes (around 3min with green tea). It's always a good
    idea to check the tea package. They usually list the optimal brew time.
  5. Enjoy your tea as it is or you can add some sugar/honey and if you want to be adventurous (and also possibly British) you can even add a bit of milk!
